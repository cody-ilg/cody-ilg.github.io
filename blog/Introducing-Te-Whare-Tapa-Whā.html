Te Whare Tapa Whā

Te Whare Tapa Whā is a model of the 4 dimensions of wellbeing developed by Tā Mason Durie in 1984 to provide a Māori perspective on health.

The 4 dimensions are: taha tinana (physical wellbeing), taha hinengaro (mental wellbeing), taha wairua (spiritual wellbeing) and taha whānau (family wellbeing). The 4 dimensions represent the 4 walls of a wharenui(communal house).

Taha Tinana (physical wellbeing)

To care for my physical wellbeing, I signed up for a gym membership when I started foundations, I plan to go atleast twice a week. During foundations I also took one short walk a day on my break, and will walk to and from bootcamp(weather permitting). I have also cut way down on alcohol and big nights out, to save more of myself for the waking hours.

Taha Hinengaro (emotional and mental wellbeing)

Taking what I learned about neuroplasticity, I will continue to make small changes to my daily habits. these include, feeling proud of myself for completing a task and taking mindfulness breaks. Instead of feeling sad over failures, I will use them as an opportunity to understand what went wrong, and think about how to avoid the same mistake.

Taha Wairua (spiritual wellbeing)

Nature is very important to my spiritual wellbeing, especially spending so much time on screens. I often go for drives on the weekend to the south coast, or kapiti to spend some time away from my usual setting.

Taha Whānau (family wellbeing)

My family lives in Wellington and I have been making an effort to see them once a week and tell them how I am going. I will spend a few nights a week staying at my partners, whom is very supportive of me.

Whenua- Interconnections to the land and environment

When I am in nature, be it just sitting or going for a walk. I make a concious effort not to check my phone and take it as a chance to really pay attention to our beautiful aoeteroa.
