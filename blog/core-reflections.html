Core Reflections, my Foundations Journey

What I learned about myself

From the core learning I became more aware of my strengths, and how I could improve on my weak points. I can be overconfident, and underestimate how much time somthing will take. I also learnt I can change my behaviour by having a 'growth mindset' and thinking positively about change.

Role of values, empathy, and self-awareness in learning and programming

Programming, like all things requires you to look after yourself and others. You need to be sure you are fair to your peers and yourself, and maintain a healthy mind, body and soul.

What suprised you most about core learning?

The amount of work needed to implement all of it into your work and life! I plan to work on the core material covered in foundation through bootcamp, and take it to the rest of my life as well.

What was challenging about core learning?

The most challenging thing for me was leaving enough time for it. I focused mainly on the tech, and if I could do it again I would have taken the core more seriously as I stand to benifit greatly from the material. In particular thinking like a programmer and mindfulness.

Why do you think we are spending so much time on core learning in a web development Bootcamp course?

Because Dev Academy aims to teach me how to be a human who can work in a team, on projects and in the workplace. Learning is code is a huge part of that, but if i dont have core human skills and self awarness it may not do me much good.

Does the time you spent studying core learning here feel like a waste of time?

No. I feel personally the core skills taught are just as important for me as the codeing. I recognise that I need the solid foundation of my self in order to learn and eventually begin my carrer in development. I have enjoyed and benefitted exploring the aspects of myself, and practising ways to better myself.
