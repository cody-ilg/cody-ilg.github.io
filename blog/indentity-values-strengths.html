Explain a situation where you have made an ethical decision.

My first car developed an 'engine rattle'. My mechanic friend informed me it was likely that the engine was on the way out which would total the car. He also mentioned he knew a method to temporarily hide the knock by putting a thicker oil in it, which would allow me to sell it for normal market value. If I listed it for sale disclosing the knock I would have gotten essentially scrap value. The benifit of doing this meant more money in my empty student pockets to buy another car. However I would know I had sold someone a financial time-bomb. Regardless of the financial position of the buyer, tricking someone like that for monetary gain does not sit right with me, and so I sold it for scrap. Even though I may have winced at the lost money, I know I made the right call according to my values.

Describe how your culture and the people around you have influenced your values and identity.

I come from a familial line of cowboys and loggers. This has given me a sense of self-reliance (perhaps to a fault). Many of my close friends I have known since highschool, and they are very empathetic and accepting to many different types of people. My friends have taught me deep and long lasting friendship is incredibly valuble, and not quickly built, as well as the beauty of diversity.

Identify your strengths and how they will support you during your learning journey.

My greatest strength is my eagerness to learn new things. I love to get lost in new concepts and exploring the ways I can implement them and shape them with my own perspective and creativity. I also consider myself a fast learner in a practical setting. Once I can get my hands on the tools I learn quickly, being able to mess around and make mistakes.

Evaluate your limitations in terms of your learning and career development. How might these affect your learning journey?

My greatest limitation is my ability to self moderate and hold myself accountable. I will often spend too much time on one thing and neglect others even if they are equally important. If I dont enjoy what I am doing I find it hard to make myself do it. This can become a big problem if I start to fall behind on a project as this compounds on itself. Aside from learning to code, teaching myself to self moderate and stay focused will be my biggest challenge.

Share an example of when you were trying to work productively with others, but there was resistance or tension.

Working as a chef at my last job, one of my seniors was pretty 'micro-managy'. While I valued their expereince, constant reminders of small mistakes I had already learned to avoid really dug at my confidence and made me less effective. After a few busy shifts I decided to talk to them one on one and voice my issues with their managment style. This worked, as we are both reasonable people and aware we have flaws. We ended up becoming a great team and even friends.
